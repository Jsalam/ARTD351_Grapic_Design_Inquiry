Hand-picked color palettes

Introduction

The color picker implemented in this web page helps designers to create color palettes for the visualization of datasets in the process of data analytics. The palettes are made of colors selected from an image and sorted by the perceived distance between them. To help the sorting task, each picked color can be visualized as one of its color components in the HSV and Lab color spaces.

We are interested in developing functional and symbolic color palettes from images linked to the actual phenomena captured in datasets. For instance, the color palette used to visualize bird migrations is crafted from actual pictures of birds. We decide not to use interpolations between colors, that is why instead of using computational tools to automatically build the palettes, we hand-pick colors following a perceptual approach.

Process

Take several photographs related to the topic of the dataset you chose for your visualization project. Compose the photos having in mind:

  • For categorical color palettes take photos with as many hues as possible
  • For sequential color palettes take photos with a few dominant hues and a wide range of saturation and lightness
  • For divergent color palettes take photos with two or three dominant hues and a wide range of saturation and lightness

Choose the photo you want to use and start picking the colors. To reduce the number of colors you can pixelate the image using Photoshop. Try different filters and evaluate the results but do not over-simplify the image. The number of resulting colors should be around 255. The crystalize filter is highly recommended,

Save the image as .png or .jpg and load it into the color picker tool

Pick the colors you want for your palette. Try to select them in order from lighter to darker or viceversa.

If you are picking colors for a categorical palette, the order does not matter, just try to pick the ones that clearly differentiate from each other

Sort the selection based on the color attributes.

Evaluate your palette in terms of value linearity and chroma diversity until you have a consistent sequence of colors.

Once you are satisfied with your palette, export it in RGB format.

This color picker tool has been tested on Chrome and Safari. Some glitches may happen in Firefox and Internet Explorer
